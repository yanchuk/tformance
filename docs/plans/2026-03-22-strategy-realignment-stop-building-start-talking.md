## Premises Challenged

### P1: "AI Detection is the wedge" → INVALID
- DNA codex core claim

Two independent problems with this premise:

**a) The detection *layer* is wrong.** Founder's own company rejected PR-level AI detection, chose usegitai.com (git-level measurement) instead. This suggests PR analysis is not the right detection surface — direct IDE/git-level tracking is more credible to buyers.

**b) Detection *feasibility* is questionable regardless of layer.** METR research shows devs perceive 24% speedup while actually being 19% slower. Self-report is unreliable. ML detection varies by vendor (Span claims 95%, but on what dataset?). No single detection method has proven trustworthy enough to anchor a product on.

- Founder already thinking differently: "correlation and different tools/sources to help eng managers"
- **New direction:** TBD pending customer conversations. Candidate wedges include: (1) reviewer bottleneck detection, (2) multi-signal team performance with AI as one dimension, (3) engineering efficiency for teams that outgrew spreadsheets but can't justify Jellyfish

### P2: "ICP is CTOs of 15-75 engineers (sweet spot 25-50)" → UNCERTAIN
- Only demand signal comes from a 100-engineer company
- CTO community respondent who showed interest wanted cross-Jira sync (complexity signal)
- ICP sizing needs validation through customer conversations before committing
- **Status:** Hypothesis, not validated profile

### P3: "Public OSS pages are primary acquisition channel" → PREMATURE
- Significant code investment in public pages (~14K lines Python + templates, plus migrations, tests, config)
- Zero signups from public pages
- Founder acknowledges pages were built "for fun" and as a vibecoding experiment
- GTM relies on this channel for 10K monthly visitors within 6 months
- **Reality:** Building-instead-of-selling pattern. Pages should come after product-market fit, not before

### P4: "Flat-rate pricing ($99/$299/$699)" → CONTRADICTED
- GTM codex is internally coherent on flat-rate tiers ($99/$299/$699), explicitly says "affordable without per-seat anxiety"
- PRD is internally coherent on per-seat ($10-25/seat)
- The conflict is between the two documents, not within either one
- Neither model validated with buyers
- **Status:** Pricing TBD, docs need alignment after customer conversations

## Recommended Approach: Minimum Viable Sales

**Stop building features. Start talking to buyers.**

### Week 1-2: Outreach Sprint
1. **Audit CTO community size first.** Determine how many relevant targets exist. If fewer than 20 qualified CTOs are accessible, supplement with LinkedIn outreach to engineering leaders at Series A-D companies with 50-200 engineers.

2. **Identify 20 CTOs** from the CTO community (or LinkedIn) who mentioned:
   - Difficulty measuring team performance
   - AI tool evaluation or renewal decisions
   - Review bottleneck complaints
   - Internal dashboard-building attempts

2. **Craft an insight-led DM** — not a pitch. Example:
   > "Hey [name], I noticed your team at [company] has been dealing with [specific signal from their public repos or community discussion]. I've been building a tool that surfaces review bottlenecks and team delivery patterns from GitHub data. Would you be open to a 15-minute look? Not selling anything — trying to understand if this solves a real problem."

3. **Demo the existing product** — you have a working dashboard, public pages with real data, OG images. Use what you've built as a conversation starter, not as the finished product.

4. **Ask the hard questions in every conversation:**
   - "If this tool disappeared tomorrow, would you notice?"
   - "What would you pay for this? Not hypothetically — would you put a credit card down today?"
   - "What's missing that would make this a must-have?"

### Week 3-4: Synthesize and Rewrite
5. **Capture every conversation** in a structured format:
   - Who (name, company, team size, stack)
   - Pain confirmed or denied
   - Willingness to pay (Y/N/conditional)
   - Feature requests
   - Objections

6. **Rewrite strategy docs** based on real data:
   - DNA codex: update wedge based on what buyers actually care about
   - ICP codex: update firmographics based on who showed genuine demand
   - GTM codex: update channels based on what conversations taught you
   - PRD: align pricing with willingness-to-pay data
   - Add usegitai.com and Milestone to competitor research

### What NOT to Do During This Sprint
- Do NOT build new features
- Do NOT rewrite the docs before conversations
- Do NOT expand integrations
- Do NOT optimize public pages for SEO
- The product is good enough for demos. The strategy is not good enough for customers.
