## GTM Thesis

Tformance should go to market by proving value before asking for trust.

The first motion is not enterprise sales. It is public proof, fast onboarding, and a narrow commercial story:

- engineering performance plus AI impact
- built for smaller engineering organizations
- affordable without per-seat anxiety
- credible because it starts from GitHub and real pull-request flow

The product wins when a buyer can move from "this public benchmark is interesting" to "I want this for my team" with as little friction as possible.

## Core Positioning

### What We Sell

Tformance helps engineering leaders answer two questions:

1. How is the team actually delivering?
2. Are AI coding tools making delivery better?

### What Makes The Positioning Work

- It is specific.
- It maps to a budget that already exists.
- It avoids broad "developer productivity platform" language.
- It gives smaller teams a reason to choose Tformance over larger, more expensive platforms.

### Messaging Guardrails

- Lead with proof, not hype.
- Put the AI ROI question next to cycle time, throughput, and review health.
- Use honest contrast with enterprise tools instead of pretending feature parity.
- Keep the commercial message simple: clear value, fast setup, sensible pricing.

## Launch Stages

### Stage 1: Discoverability And Proof

Goal: create awareness and trust with assets that compound.

Primary motions:
- public OSS org and repo pages
- SEO and GEO for engineering metrics and AI-adoption queries
- comparison pages versus known competitors
- clear product pages that explain the GitHub-first motion

Success signal:
- relevant inbound traffic from technical buyers
- early signups from visitors who already understand the product story

### Stage 2: Founder-Led Conversion

Goal: turn proof into design partners and early customers.

Primary motions:
- founder-led outbound using benchmark observations and category insight
- warm introductions through peers, investors, and operator networks
- community participation in engineering leadership circles
- targeted outreach to teams visibly experimenting with AI coding tools

Success signal:
- repeatable conversations with qualified buyers
- first paying customers who match the intended ICP

### Stage 3: Repeatable Product-Led Growth

Goal: make the self-serve motion stronger while keeping selective assisted help for larger or more strategic accounts.

Primary motions:
- smoother signup-to-dashboard path
- stronger conversion from public proof to trial
- lifecycle messaging around first value, retention, and expansion
- selective assisted onboarding for higher-value teams

Success signal:
- increasing share of customers converting through a repeatable path
- expansion driven by deeper product usage, not just sales effort

## Acquisition Channels

### 1. Public OSS Pages

This is the most distinctive top-of-funnel asset in the repo today.

Use public repo and org pages to:
- rank for long-tail engineering metrics searches
- create citable benchmark content for AI search engines
- show buyers what Tformance looks like before login
- give outbound and social sharing a concrete landing surface

### 2. Comparison Content

Use competitor comparison pages to capture buyers already evaluating alternatives. The tone should stay direct and honest, especially where larger tools are stronger.

### 3. Founder-Led Outbound

Outbound should start from insight, not spam. The best message is a useful observation about review flow, AI adoption, or benchmark context that makes the recipient curious enough to look.

### 4. Community And Content

Participate where engineering leaders already ask these questions:
- engineering leadership communities
- AI tooling discussions
- CTO and VP Engineering peer groups
- practical content on AI ROI, review pressure, and delivery health

### 5. Customer Stories And Benchmarks

As soon as credible customers exist, reuse their outcomes as proof. Real stories will convert better than category-level claims.

## Partner And Distribution Strategy

The near-term partner motion should stay lightweight and practical.

### Best-Fit Partners

- fractional CTOs and engineering advisors
- GitHub and Jira consultants
- engineering leadership communities and operator networks
- AI tooling consultants helping teams roll out Copilot- or Cursor-class products

### Why These Partners

They already sit near the moment when the buyer asks:
"How do we know whether this tooling or process change is helping?"

### What Not To Do Yet

- do not build a heavyweight channel program
- do not depend on formal platform partnerships to start distribution
- do not spread the team across too many co-marketing experiments before the core message converts

## Handoff Model

The handoff model should reflect a hybrid motion: product-led by default, founder-assisted when valuable.

### Self-Serve Path

1. Prospect lands on a public benchmark, comparison page, or product page.
2. Prospect signs up and connects GitHub.
3. Tformance delivers first dashboard value quickly.
4. Product prompts the next step: select repos, complete sync, optionally add Jira and Slack.
5. Prospect sees enough signal to continue trial and convert.

### Assisted Path

1. Prospect arrives through outbound, referral, or a higher-intent conversation.
2. Founder or product lead gives a short demo anchored in the buyer's AI and delivery questions.
3. Prospect is guided through GitHub-first setup.
4. First findings are reviewed together.
5. Trial converts into a paid plan, with expansion based on richer usage and context needs.

### Internal Rule

Sales should not outrun onboarding. The handoff is successful only when the buyer reaches first value quickly and understands what the product will deepen next.

## Pricing And Monetization

### Current Commercial Model

Pricing should stay anchored to the approved flat-rate structure:

| Plan | Team Size | Price |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Trial | Any | $0 |
| Starter | Up to 10 developers | $99/mo |
| Team | 11-50 developers | $299/mo |
| Business | 51-150 developers | $699/mo |
| Enterprise | 150+ | Custom |

Annual pricing should continue to offer two months free.

### Why This Model Fits The GTM

- it lowers friction for smaller teams
- it avoids per-seat anxiety as adoption grows
- it supports self-serve evaluation
- it reinforces the position that Tformance is a practical alternative to expensive enterprise tools

### Expansion Paths

Future monetization can deepen without replacing the flat-rate model:

- premium Jira enrichment and deeper context
- advanced insight and recommendation layers
- enterprise packaging around support, security, and governance
- higher-touch onboarding or services where strategically useful

## Near-Term GTM Milestones

### Milestone 1: Sharpen The Story

- finalize internal strategy docs and positioning
- align public pages, comparison content, and product copy with the same message
- ensure pricing and onboarding story are easy to understand

### Milestone 2: Turn Public Proof Into Pipeline

- expand and refine public OSS repo and org pages
- improve conversion paths from benchmark content to signup
- use benchmark content in outbound and community conversations

### Milestone 3: Win The First Repeatable Customers

- close early customers that fit the ICP
- capture why they bought, what they trusted, and what almost blocked the deal
- turn those learnings into onboarding, messaging, and retention improvements

## Risks And Watchouts

- Public traffic may be high quality for SEO but weak for conversion if the commercial handoff is vague.
- AI detection claims will backfire if confidence and limitations are not explained clearly.
- The product can lose trust quickly if it feels like surveillance or shallow ranking.
- The team can get pulled upmarket too early by enterprise requests that distort the core product.
- Pricing that is too cheap can attract interest but under-signal seriousness; pricing changes should follow proof, not guesswork.
- GTM will stay noisy unless onboarding makes first value obvious within the first session or first few minutes.

## Strategic Constraint

Tformance should not try to win by matching every feature of larger competitors. It should win by being clearer, faster to adopt, more affordable, and more focused on the AI-and-delivery question the first buyer actually has.
